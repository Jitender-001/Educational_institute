Bachelor of Laws (LLB)

The Bachelor of Laws (LLB) program is an undergraduate degree program designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of legal principles, theories, and practices. The program typically spans three to five years, depending on the jurisdiction, and equips students with the knowledge and skills necessary for a career in law or related fields.

Core Courses

  • Introduction to Law/li>
  • Legal Methods and Research
  • Constitutional Law
  • Contract Law
  • Property Law
  • Criminal Law
  • Administrative Law
  • Human Rights Law
  • Legal Ethics and Professional Responsibility
  • Labour and Employment Law

Clinical Legal Education

Many LLB programs offer clinical legal education components where students gain practical experience through internships, legal aid clinics, or moot court competitions, allowing them to apply legal principles in real-world scenarios.

Final Year Dissertation or Project:

In the final year, students may undertake a dissertation or research project on a legal topic of their choice under the supervision of faculty members.

Fee Structure

  • Tuition Fee: $1000 per semester
  • Registration Fee: $500 (one-time payment)
  • Examination Fee: $200 per semester
  • Bar Association Membership Fee: $500 (annual payment)
  • Library Fee: $20 per semester
  • Miscellaneous Fee (including student activities, insurance, etc.): $100 per semester
