Course Details

Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS)

The Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) program is a rigorous undergraduate degree program designed to prepare students for a career in medicine. Over the course of five to six years, students undergo comprehensive training in medical sciences, clinical skills, and patient care, equipping them with the knowledge and expertise necessary to become competent medical practitioners.

Program Structure

  • Pre-clinical Phase (Years 1-2):
    • Foundations of Medical Sciences: Anatomy, Physiology, Biochemistry, and Histology.
    • Introduction to Medical Practice: Medical Ethics, Professionalism, and Communication Skills.
    • Introduction to Clinical Skills: Basic Examination Techniques and Patient Interaction.
  • Clinical Phase (Years 3-5)
    • Clinical Rotations: Internal Medicine, Surgery, Pediatrics, Obstetrics and Gynecology, Psychiatry, and Family Medicine.
    • Clinical Skills Development: Advanced Examination Techniques, Diagnostic Procedures, and Treatment Modalities.
    • Clerkships: Hands-on experience in various clinical settings, including hospitals, clinics, and community health centers.

Elective Rotations (Optional)

In the final year, students may have the opportunity to pursue elective rotations in specialized fields of medicine, such as Cardiology, Neurology, Orthopedics, or Dermatology, to further explore their areas of interest./p>

Internship (Compulsory):

After completing the academic curriculum, students are required to undergo a one-year internship in a hospital or healthcare facility, where they gain practical experience under the supervision of licensed physicians.

Licensing Examination

Upon successful completion of the MBBS program and internship, graduates are eligible to take the licensing examination to become registered medical practitioners.

Fee Structure

  • Tuition Fee: $1000 per semester
  • Registration Fee: $500 (one-time payment)
  • Examination Fee: $200 per semester
  • Laboratory Fee: $50 per semester
  • Clinical Training Fee: $100 per academic year
  • Miscellaneous Fee (including student activities, insurance, etc.): $100 per semester
